The plan

Consolidate

You already have all the pieces but they are on separate islands. The Institute is the trunk that connects them, one path from a single episode to a daily practice to a community to the land.

01 · NOW

Stage one connects what exists

One front door, one login, and a community space. The podcast, Insight Timer, Substack, Patreon, and Roots to Sky keep their jobs and simply point home. I made a demo of what this could look like here.

02 · DAY ONE

The sangha is open and two-way

A members' commons where people can post, reply to each other, and discuss connected to episodes, Insight Timer links, Substack posts, so comments live with the content that sparked them.

03 · LATER

Courses come later, on WordPress

Discrete, self-paced courses of your own design or co-developed with collaborators, podcast guests, and other space-holders. Your Patreon can unlock them by tier, so patrons sign up once and gain access.

Where it lives

Build it on WordPress

One home holds the whole ecosystem. My recommendation is WordPress, because it keeps the community and, later, the courses under one roof you own. Circle is a strong alternative if you would rather have the most turnkey community tool from day one.

🌐 WordPress (recommended)

Why
You own the members, the content, and the domain. The community runs on a plugin such as BuddyBoss, and future courses live here too, gated by your Patreon tiers.
Cost
Roughly $300 to $600 per year in plugins, on hosting you already pay for. No per-member fees beyond Stripe or Patreon.

🔥 Circle (alternative)

A turnkey community, leased not owned
Why
The sangha, live circles, and payments share one roof with nothing to maintain, and member-to-member conversation is built in.
Cost
Roughly $1,100 to $2,400 per year (the $89 to $199 per month Professional and Business plans, billed annually), plus 0.5 to 2 percent transaction fees on top of Stripe. The home is leased, and courses would still be built on WordPress, so this path means two connected homes later.
Trade
The best community tool now, but in exchange for a subscription and less ownership.
The short of it: WordPress if ownership and one roof matter most, Circle if the turnkey community matters most. The full comparison, costs, community mechanics, and the Patreon setup steps are in the detailed companion.
