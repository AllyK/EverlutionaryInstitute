Where it lives

A community platform, not an LMS

A traditional LMS puts the course catalog at the center and bolts community on: the cold-catalog feel this whole design avoids. Three of the five ideas (the fire keepers, the sangha open from day one, the return to the land) are community and events work, not courseware. The home needs to hold membership, spaces, live events, and modular courses together. My recommendation: pilot on Circle, and keep everything you already use in its current job. Here is how it compares to the strongest alternatives: Mighty Networks (the other community-first home), Kajabi (the all-in-one marketing suite), and the LMS route (Thinkific or Teachable).
